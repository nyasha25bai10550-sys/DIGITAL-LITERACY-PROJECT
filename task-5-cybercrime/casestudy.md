Part A – Case Study (Phishing)

Title: Phishing Attack on College Students

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information, such as passwords, bank details, or personal identity data. Typically, phishing occurs through emails, messages, or fake websites that look legitimate.

How it happens (step-by-step):
	1.	The attacker sends an email or WhatsApp message claiming to be from a bank, college, or popular app.
	2.	The message contains a link asking the recipient to “verify” their account or claim a reward.
	3.	The link leads to a fake website that looks identical to the real one.
	4.	The victim enters login credentials or UPI/credit card information.
	5.	The attacker captures the information and uses it to steal money or access accounts.

Targets: College students are common targets because they are active online, often less cautious, and may share sensitive data on social media.

Consequences: Victims may lose money, have accounts hacked, or face identity theft. Recovery can be difficult and time-consuming, sometimes affecting credit scores or personal reputation.

Source/Reference: Inspired by real incidents reported on the [National Cyber Crime Portal] (https://cybercrime.gov.in).
